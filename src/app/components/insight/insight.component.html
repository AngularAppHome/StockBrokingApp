Chart of The Day

Most interesting charts with insights

  • Credit Flow To MSME Sector Improves

    21st August 2018 10

    The credit flow to the Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSME) sector seems to have finally started recovering after taking a big hit during demonetisation, as per a study by RBI. Credit to micro and small enterprises  grew by 0.71% Y-o-Y, while credit to Medium enterprises grew 2.69% Y-o-Y in June-18.

    Insight: The MSME sector employs about 111 million people, and accounts for around 30% of India's GDP. They are major component of India's exports with a 40% share. Slowdown in credit to the sector along with issues related to GST implementation were a major source of weakness in the sector. As seen through IIP as well, the sector has been declining for 17 straight months.

  • Rainfall Distribution Continues To Be A Risk

    20th August 2018 26

    As of the week ended Aug-15, 2018, South-west monsoon was 5.8% below its normal level. Given that the IMD defines normal rains as between 96% and 104% of a long period average - we are currently having a below-normal rainfall.

    Insight: 24 of the 36 sub-divisions have received below-normal rainfall. While regions like Kerala have been devasted with 255.4% excess rainfall, many interior regions like Rayalseema and Marathwada are rain-starved. The uneven spatial distribution of rainfall poses a concern for the rural economy as well as inflation going ahead.

  • Commercial Vehicles Sales Remain Healthy

    17th August 2018 21

    Commercial vehicles segment continued to post healthy sales numbers, with total domestic sales growing by 29.7% Y-o-Y in July. M&HCV sales grew by 24.5% Y-o-Y and LCV sales grew by 32.9% Y-o-Y.

    Insight: Although the growth has moderated as compared to last month, the high double digit growth is still maintained in both M&HCV and LCV. Growth came on the back of strong demand from infrastructure and construction sectors, rural demand recovery and rising shipments from e-commerce industry. This comes despite the new axle load norms, the impact of which might be more visible in the coming months.

  • Trade Deficit Widens To A 5-Year High

    16th August 2018 22

    Trade deficit in July widened to a 5-year high of USD 18 Billion from USD 17.1 Billion in June. Exports were USD 25.8 Billion and imports were USD 43.8 Billion.

    Insight: The export growth was weak on the back of tepid growth in petroleum products as well as as moderated growth in non-POL exports despite a low base. Threatened global trade flows amidst the looming trade tensions, might be one factor for a weak performance.  As for imports, robust growth in electronics goods imports was seen. Going forward, global geopolitics especially as regards to oil, might impact the imports.

  • Food Inflation Softens Sharply

    14th August 2018 15

    Slowing food inflation was a major factor behind moderation in headline inflation in July. Food inflation came in at 1.4% Y-o-Y in July as against 2.9% Y-o-Y in June.

    Insight: Food Inflation moderated on the back of deflation in vegetable prices and stable cereal prices. Vegetables prices saw a deflation of 2.2% Y-o-Y in July, mainly on the back of a rising base. Cereal prices have stayed in 2-3% range for the last 8 months, still not reflecting the hike in MSP prices. Going ahead MSP price hike and weak spatial distribution of rainfall might be an upside risk.
